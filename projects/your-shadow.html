Published Project

Your Shadow

Solo horror prototype shipped in roughly two weeks. The project combines procedural map generation, navigation pressure, and stalking AI behavior where enemies advance primarily when the player is not looking.

Status: Published | Team Size: Solo | Timeline: 2 Weeks
Play on itch.io

Overview

Your Shadow was designed as a compact horror experiment where uncertainty and pursuit behavior do most of the emotional work. Each run starts with a new map layout and enemy approach vectors, keeping memorization from flattening tension.

Core Systems

Random Map Generation

Room and corridor permutations produce unfamiliar pathing each run.

Enemy Navigation

Enemies route through generated spaces with fallback behavior in tight geometry.

Stalking Logic

Enemies close distance when the player camera is not focused on them.

Pacing Controls

Spawn and approach timing tuned to sustain pressure without exhausting players.

Stalking Behavior Model

Enemy Evaluates Player Visibility
Player Looking At Enemy?
Yes -> Hold or Slow
No -> Advance Along Navigation Path
Recheck Visibility

The key tension mechanism is not jump-scares. It is visibility-gated movement that manipulates trust in the player camera.

What I Learned From Shipping Fast

  • Strong behavior rules can outperform heavy content volume in horror prototypes.
  • Simple procedural generation pays off immediately for replay value.
  • When scope is tight, system clarity matters more than visual complexity.
