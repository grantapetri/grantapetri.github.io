How I Think

Engineering Philosophy

I approach gameplay development as systems engineering. I am less interested in one-off features and more interested in building frameworks that are maintainable, scalable, and fast to iterate on.

Building Reusable Systems

I prioritize reusable gameplay systems over one-off implementations so features can be expanded without accumulating duplicate logic.

Data-Driven Architecture

I separate configuration data from runtime behavior. This keeps systems adaptable and lets gameplay tuning happen without rewriting core logic.

Configuration Over Hardcoding

If behavior can be expressed through parameters, I expose it through configuration. Hardcoded behavior is a last resort for stable constraints.

Modular Design

I split large gameplay controllers into focused modules. Clear module boundaries make debugging, testing, and team collaboration easier.

Iteration Speed Is A Feature

Good architecture makes iteration faster. Systems that are painful to tune slow down design quality and reduce experimentation.

Engineering Solves Gameplay Problems

I treat engineering as a gameplay tool: balancing responsiveness and authority, readability and complexity, flexibility and maintainability.

Continuous Learning Through Refactoring

I regularly refactor when systems reveal architectural friction. Refactoring is not rework; it is how strong systems evolve.

Personal Statement

I enjoy solving gameplay problems through engineering and building systems that are maintainable, scalable, and enjoyable to iterate on.
