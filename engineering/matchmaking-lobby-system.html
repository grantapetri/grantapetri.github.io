Engineering Case Study

Matchmaking & Lobby System

A multiplayer session flow that handles loadouts, queueing, match starts, and clean transitions from prep space to active gameplay.

Core Focus

Match FlowQueueingPlayer Readiness

1. Overview

The matchmaking and lobby system organizes the player journey before the match even begins. It gives players a place to manage loadouts, enter queues, wait for sessions to form, and transition into the game without confusion or broken state.

2. Design Goals

  • Create a reliable flow from lobby to active match.
  • Keep queue and readiness state easy to reason about.
  • Support future scaling for larger session rules or party systems.

3. Architecture

The system separates pre-match player state from live match state. Queue management, loadout choices, and teleport or spawn transitions are handled as explicit phases, which makes it easier to recover from cancellations, disconnects, or partial fills.

4. Implementation

Players enter the lobby, configure equipment, and signal intent to queue. Once the server decides the session is ready, players are moved into the active match and the game timer starts. By structuring each step as a known phase, it becomes easier to lock or unlock actions based on where the player is in the flow.

5. Challenges & Solutions

Match flow systems can break down when players disconnect, cancel, or change state at awkward times. I approached that by giving the server clear ownership over readiness and queue transitions, which prevents clients from drifting into invalid session states.

6. Results / Lessons Learned

The result was a cleaner onboarding flow for matches and a better foundation for multiplayer scaling. The key lesson was that session UX depends heavily on state clarity, not just on the teleport or spawn logic itself.
