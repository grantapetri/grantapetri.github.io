Engineering Case Study

Procedural Map Generation

A seeded generation pipeline built to create replayable layouts while preserving tension curves, navigability, and encounter structure.

Core Focus

Procedural GenerationLevel FlowReplayability

1. Overview

Procedural Map Generation was built to give Site 23 replayable spaces without losing atmosphere or pacing. The goal was not randomness for its own sake, but controllable variation that still produces useful exploration and encounter opportunities.

2. Design Goals

  • Create fresh runs through seeded layout variation.
  • Preserve readability and progression even when rooms are rearranged.
  • Support thematic and gameplay constraints during generation.

3. Architecture

The system works from modular building pieces, generation rules, and seeded randomness. Layout selection, adjacency constraints, and content placement operate in stages so the generator can reject invalid results early and preserve overall flow.

4. Implementation

I structured the generator as a pipeline: choose a seed, assemble valid modules, enforce connectivity, then place encounter or atmosphere elements on top. This allows level logic to evolve independently from raw room assets and makes debugging far easier than a single opaque generation pass.

5. Challenges & Solutions

The difficult balance was between variation and quality control. I handled that by constraining the problem with modular rules and post-generation validation rather than trying to hand-author every possible outcome. That preserved replayability without creating obviously broken layouts.

6. Results / Lessons Learned

The system made replayable horror spaces more feasible while still respecting pacing goals. The major lesson was that procedural generation works best when it is treated as authored possibility space, not unrestricted randomness.
